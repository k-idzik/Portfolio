MOONMAN

MoonMan is a project I worked on for an Interactive Media class. MoonMan was implemented in Processing in around a week, and was designed to include general random algorithms, Perlin noise, and Gaussian distribution. The general goal of MoonMan is to avoid being hit by the oncoming rocks, which will speed up as the game progresses. Players can either jump over the rocks by holding down the spacebar, or shoot the rock by pressing enter. Shooting the rock will net you 100 points, but while jumping over the rock awards you no points, it could be useful as you must reload between shots. Getting hit by the rock will remove 250 points from your score, whereas holding down the spacebar in an attempt to outsmart the oncoming rocks will loose you one point per frame you are at the top of the MoonMan's jump. I'd like to revisit this project in the future, where I'd rework jumping to work in a more traditional manner and add other mechanics to make the game more interesting. If you'd like to play the game for yourself, two Windows executables are provided below. Java is required to play the game.
